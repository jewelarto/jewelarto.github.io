10 Rare and Fascinating Words That Start With R!

Words That Start with R The English language is full of words that start with the letter ‘R’. These words can be used to describe many things, and can be used in all sorts of contexts.

From everyday objects to animals, emotions and more - here are some of the most commonly used words that start with the letter 'R'. Rabbit: A small, furry mammal with distinctive long ears and a short tail. Rabbits are found in many parts of the world, and are often kept as pets. Rain: Water that falls from the sky in droplets. Rain is essential for life on earth, as it helps to water plants and replenish water supplies. Red: The colour of fire, love and passion. Red is also associated with danger and intensity. Run: To move quickly on foot over a short distance. Running is a great way to stay fit and healthy, while also being enjoyable! Rescue: To save someone or something from harm or an unpleasant situation. Rescue operations can involve anything from helping animals stuck in a tree to rescuing people trapped in a burning building. Religion: A set of beliefs about life, death and the universe which many people follow faithfully throughout their lives. The two major monotheistic religions are Christianity and Islam, but there are many others around the world too. Respect: To have admiration for someone or something because they have done something worthy or admirable. Respect can also refer to treating someone kindly regardless of their background or beliefs - an important quality to have in life! Rhythm: A regular pattern of beats or sounds which may be used as part of music or dance performances, as well as certain spoken languages such as French or Spanish where certain syllables may be emphasised more than others when speaking them out loud. Resilience: The ability to withstand stress or misfortune without breaking down emotionally or physically; often referred to as 'bouncing back' after a difficult event or period in one's life. Individuals who possess resilience tend to be able to cope better with difficulties than those who do not possess this trait. Rescue Dog: Dogs that are specially trained by organisations such as fire services and search-and-rescue teams for activities such as locating lost individuals or those trapped under rubble following natural disasters; these dogs often possess amazing levels of intelligence, loyalty and courage which make them invaluable members of these teams! These are just some examples of words that start with the letter ‘R’; there are hundreds more out there waiting for you to discover!
