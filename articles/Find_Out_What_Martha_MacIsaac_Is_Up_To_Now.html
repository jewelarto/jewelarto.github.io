Find Out What Martha MacIsaac Is Up To Now!

Martha MacIsaac is an actress from Canada best known for her breakout roles in the cult comedy Superbad and the horror film remake of House of Wax. She has also had roles in the television shows Supernatural, Nikita, and Shooter.

Born in 1984 in Antigonish, Nova Scotia, MacIsaac grew up with a strong interest in performing. She began her acting career when she was still a teenager, appearing in several Canadian television series including Wind at My Back and Da Vinci’s Inquest before moving on to bigger projects. MacIsaac’s breakthrough role was as the love interest of Michael Cera’s character Evan in the 2007 comedy Superbad. The movie was a hit with both critics and audiences alike and earned MacIsaac considerable recognition. She followed up this success by appearing alongside Paris Hilton and Chad Michael Murray in the 2005 remake of House of Wax as Carly Jones. MacIsaac was praised for her performance as the naive yet brave protagonist who must find her way out of a dangerous wax museum filled with malicious figures. Since then, MacIsaac has had numerous roles on both television and film. She is well-known for her recurring role as Ellie Granger on the supernatural drama series Supernatural and had a lead role as Sonya on The CW's short-lived series Nikita from 2010 to 2013. MacIsaac also starred alongside Ryan Phillippe on USA Network's Shooter from 2016 to 2018 playing Mary Swagger, wife of Phillipe's character Bob Lee Swagger. In addition to acting, MacIsaac is an active humanitarian who has worked with many charities throughout her career. She recently appeared on a panel discussion sponsored by UN Women Canada which aimed to raise awareness about gender equality among local students at Ryerson University. MacIsaac has established herself as one of Canada's most versatile performers over the past decade and continues to impress audiences with her work both onscreen and offscreen.
